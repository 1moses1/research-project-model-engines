We are pleased to submit the \textbf{major revision} of our manuscript,
\textbf{``AI-Augmented Compliance Auditing for Cloud Systems: A Hybrid ML--LLM Approach''}
(Manuscript ID: futureinternet-4270373), in response to the decision letter dated
25 April 2026.

We thank all three reviewers for their thorough and constructive feedback. Their combined
comments have substantially strengthened the empirical foundation of the paper, and we
believe the revised manuscript is meaningfully improved across every dimension they raised.

\textbf{The most consequential change in this revision} is the full identification and
correction of a critical data leakage issue. A systematic audit of the original training
corpus found that \textbf{86.3\%} of synthetic training rows contained embedded compliance
labels in the \texttt{log\_message} field, causing the classifier to memorize label strings
rather than learn security semantics. This produced the previously reported 99.99\% F1
score, which we explicitly retract in the revised manuscript. The synthetic data generator
was rewritten from scratch, and the corrected \textbf{XGBoost v2 achieves 85.45\% macro-F1}
(Wilson 95\% CI: [84.9\%, 86.0\%]; 5-fold CV F1: 85.20\% $\pm$ 0.25\%) on a
verified leakage-free corpus of 150{,}000 training rows---an honest estimate that falls
squarely within the paper's target range and is consistent with the feature complexity.

Beyond this central correction, the revision addresses all reviewer comments through the
following substantive changes:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Statistical rigor}: Wilson 95\% confidence intervals are now reported for
        every result in every table. The phrase ``up to 100\% accuracy'' has been removed
        throughout; all 100\% detection rates are accompanied by Wilson CIs.

  \item \textbf{LLM evaluation expanded}: Sample size increased from $n$=50 to $n$=200 per
        log type ($n$=628 total; HTTP is limited to $n$=28 real SecRepo Squid proxy
        logs---the only available real enterprise HTTP log source). GPT-4o-mini macro
        accuracy: \textbf{92.3\%} across four log types (37.5\% real enterprise data).
        Llama-3.2-3B was removed as it was underpowered for zero-shot compliance tasks.

  \item \textbf{Adversarial validation expanded}: From 2 to \textbf{5 MITRE ATT\&CK v14
        scenarios} (T1059.001, T1190, T1110, T1078, T1562.001), with macro detection rate
        \textbf{92.8\%} and 0.0\% false-positive rate on real SSH/PAM compliant logs
        ($n$=75). Each scenario is mapped to its corresponding RWNCSA control and NIST
        SP~800-53 reference.

  \item \textbf{Standardized joint evaluation}: A new Hybrid System Combined Evaluation
        (Section~4.4) assesses all three system configurations---XGBoost alone,
        GPT-4o-mini alone, Hybrid---on the \textit{identical} 180 real-world logs using
        the same metrics and ground truth. XGBoost alone: 62.4\% F1 (54.5\% FPR);
        GPT-4o-mini alone: 90.8\% F1 (6.4\% FPR); Hybrid combined: \textbf{85.1\% F1
        (6.4\% FPR)}.

  \item \textbf{Theoretical formulation clarified}: All variables in the effectiveness
        function $C(k) = \alpha D_k + \beta V_k + \gamma R_k$ are now explicitly defined
        with default weights ($\alpha$=0.5, $\beta$=0.3, $\gamma$=0.2) and experimental
        grounding. Multi-label output dimensionality corrected to 169 controls throughout.

  \item \textbf{Overstated claims moderated}: ``First'', ``proves'', ``strictly more
        expressive'', and comparative cost-efficiency claims have been softened or removed.
        All conclusions include explicit scope qualifiers referencing the evaluation context.

  \item \textbf{Reproducibility}: A full supplementary appendix documents dataset
        provenance, hyperparameter tables, evaluation protocols, and phase-by-phase
        experimental logs.
\end{itemize}

We note one data-size clarification for editorial awareness: the reviewer responses
reference $n$=628 total for the LLM evaluation (SSH=200, Windows=200, macOS=200,
HTTP=28). An earlier draft of the responses used $n$=800 before the HTTP sample was
constrained to real enterprise data; all final manuscript tables and the supplementary
appendix correctly reflect $n$=628.

The revised manuscript remains original, is not under review elsewhere, uses only
publicly available datasets, and complies fully with MDPI's ethical guidelines. The
Article Processing Charge is understood and accepted.

We are grateful for the opportunity to revise and resubmit, and we hope the revised
manuscript meets the standards of \textit{Future Internet}.
