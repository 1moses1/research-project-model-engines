We are pleased to submit our manuscript, \textbf{``AI-Augmented Compliance Auditing for
Cloud Systems: A Hybrid ML--LLM Approach,''} for consideration in \textit{Future Internet}.

This work starts from a question that practitioners across Africa increasingly face: what
happens when a security control passes a compliance audit, yet fails in practice against a
real attack? We found the answer uncomfortable. Using Rwanda's national cybersecurity
framework as a case study, we built and evaluated a system that tests not whether controls
exist, but whether they work.

The central finding is a tension that we believe will resonate with your readership: a
classifier achieving near-perfect accuracy on structured logs collapses entirely when it
encounters a log type it has never seen before. Resolving this tension led us through a
three-phase architectural journey, culminating in a hybrid system where machine learning and
large language models each do what the other cannot. The result is an auditor that runs on
commodity hardware at \$50/month and catches compliance failures that a checkbox audit
would certify as passing.

We believe this work speaks directly to \textit{Future Internet}'s focus on AI-driven
security and regulatory compliance in connected environments. It contributes both a rigorous
empirical evaluation and a deployable open-source system designed for institutions that
cannot afford enterprise solutions.

The manuscript is original, has not been submitted elsewhere, and is not under review at
any other journal. All datasets used are publicly available. The work complies fully with
MDPI's ethical guidelines, and the Article Processing Charge is understood and accepted.

We thank the editors and reviewers for their time and consideration.
